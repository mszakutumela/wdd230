Tshwane Chamber of Commerce Site-Plan

Site Name: Tshwane Chamber of Commerce

The chamber operates in the city of Tshwane (Pretoria), South Africa. The jacaranda tree is one of the major symbols of the city. Jacaranda trees are native to South America and first came to Tshwane (Pretoria) in 1888. A travelling nurseryman called Templeman sold two Jacaranda seedlings to Jacob Cilliers, who resided at Myrtle Lodge in Tshwane (Pretoria). A plaque was also placed at the site to commemmorate the event. The plaque was unveiled on, 8 November 1939. It is said that many of the jacaranda trees in the city originated from these two seedlings.

Site Purpose

The Tshwane Chamber of Commerce's goal is to provide relevant information about the city of Tshwane, it's businesses, people, attractions and parks. Our aim is to help local businesses, the community and visitors to become more involved and provide a platform where all parties can interact. We hope to bring the community of Tshwane together and improve the city's economy.
